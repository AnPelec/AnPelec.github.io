Research Reports

  • I tried to augment the algorithms of Bernstein and Chechik ([BC18]) and Bhattacharya and Kulkarni ([BK18]) by extending their hitting set construction to multiple levels. The goal was to use a logarithmic number of hitting sets in order to partition the vertices into buckets of constant size. Unfortunately this idea fails from two levels. In this report I describe the algorithm and provide counterexamples on why it cannot improve the running time of the original approaches.
  • Work done while working under the supervision of Prof. Virginia Vassilevska Williams
  • Project Report
  • Final Project for 6.890.
  • After describing an incremental version of Cohen's Size Estimation Framework ([Coh97]), I combined this framework with the idea of partitioning the vertices of the graph into buckets. Turns out that the estimation framework gives a better partitioning of the vertices, but still not enough to provide a polynomial speedup in the running time.
  • Work done while working under the supervision of Prof. Virginia Vassilevska Williams
